Three distinct mechanisms govern how market participants pay for and receive payment for gas in the Declared Wholesale Gas Market.

Each schedule sets an ex ante market price and scheduled quantities for each MP. Deviations, imbalances and LPA are all measured against these scheduled quantities.
// The three settlement mechanisms
01 ——
Deviation
Payment
You didn't flow what AEMO scheduled you for — in the short direction
Triggered when
actual inject < scheduled inject
— or —
actual withdraw > scheduled withdraw
Direction
Shortfall only (asymmetric)
Rate
Penalty rate or market price
Purpose
Incentivise schedule adherence
Scope
Per MP, per scheduling interval
02 ——
Imbalance
Payment
Your scheduled injections and withdrawals don't net to zero — including excess
Triggered when
scheduled inject ≠ scheduled withdraw
— or —
actual inject > scheduled inject
Direction
Both (pay or receive)
Rate
Market price (no penalty)
Purpose
Settle MP-to-MP gas trades
Scope
Per MP, across the gas day
03 ——
Linepack
Account
The net system-level pipeline storage change, socialised across all withdrawing MPs
Allocation formula
MP share = MP withdrawals
÷ total system withdrawals
× daily LPA value
Direction
Both (credit or charge)
Rate
Weighted avg market price
Purpose
Settle AEMO's linepack management
Scope
System-wide, demand-weighted

⌀
The settlement is always revenue-neutral for AEMO

The three mechanisms work together so AEMO's net position across all payments is zero on every gas day. Deviation payments penalise MPs who fail to follow their schedules. Imbalance payments settle the bilateral gas trades that result from MPs having different scheduled positions. LPA closes the loop — whatever pipeline storage AEMO deliberately built or drew down is socialised back to the market, allocated purely by each MP's share of demand. An MP in perfect balance still pays LPA. It is the only mechanism with no behavioural lever.
