02 / Physical Infrastructure

The Assets Underpinning PEC

🔌
PEC Stage 1 (PEC-1) — SA Section
Robertstown (SA) to Red Cliffs (Vic border area) — the SA leg. Currently in service at 150 MW in both directions. Registered under the existing V-SA framework until 1 Oct 2026.
🏗️
PEC Stage 2 (PEC-2) — NSW Section
~700 km of new 330 kV line from Red Cliffs/Buronga to Dinawan (near Wagga Wagga, NSW). Construction nearing completion. Adds the NSW end of the interconnector. Expected AEMO asset-ready: end Oct 2026.
⚙️
5× Phase Shifting Transformers (PSTs) — Buronga
Located at the Buronga substation (NSW). Balance power flows between PEC and Heywood by shifting the phase angle of voltage. Each tap change moves PEC flow by ~10 MW (PEC-1) or ~20 MW (PEC-2). Operated in Auto mode under normal conditions — triggers when one interconnector exceeds 80% of its limit while the other is below 70%. Manually controlled by Transgrid under AEMO instruction in high-risk conditions.
🛡️
SAIT-RAS — SA Interconnector Trip Remedial Action Scheme
A fast-acting protection scheme run by ElectraNet. If one interconnector trips under high transfer conditions, the other could overload — risking SA islanding and a state-wide blackout. SAIT-RAS can dispatch up to 600 MW of fast response in SA (load shedding, generation trip, or BESS charge/discharge) to arrest any cascade. SA BESS operators must be able to remain connected and respond predictably.
⚡ Impact on Heywood (V-SA)

Heywood remains in service at ±650 MW. PEC does not replace it — they operate in parallel. However, constraint equations for Heywood outages will be updated (removal of some FCAS limits), and the PSTs at Buronga actively divert flows between the two interconnectors to keep both within limits. The combined transfer limit is asymmetric: +1,300 MW (SA import) and −1,450 MW (SA export) — reflecting differences in thermal and stability limits in each direction.

04 / Dispatch & Forecasting

How Flows Are Calculated

Loop Flow Constraints ("L" prefix)
PEC creates a loop — power can travel SA→NSW or SA→VIC→NSW simultaneously. NEMDE's hub-and-spoke model doesn't natively model this, so new loop constraint equations are introduced.

These are prefixed "L" and are always invoked (always binding) from 1 Oct 2026. They change form depending on which network elements are in service (e.g. outages on SW NSW or Nth Vic lines). A guide: if V-SA, Murraylink or Broken Hill Solar appear on the LHS of an existing constraint, expect a PEC term to be added.
PASA & ST PASA
Short-term and medium-term PASA will be updated to model PEC-2 from September 2026 (constraints loaded ahead of go-live).

Loop flow equations are not needed in the new PASA engine — only in NEMDE dispatch and pre-dispatch. MLF calculations will include both PEC flows and a loop flow equation from registration.

📐 PST Control Logic — Dispatch Relevance

The PSTs at Buronga shift flows between HIC and PEC automatically but are not directly controllable by the market — they respond to AEMO-telemetered limit signals via ICCP. In Auto mode, the PST tap moves when one interconnector is above 80% of its telemetered limit while the other is below 70%. Each tap is ~20 MW of PEC flow. In Non-Auto (manual) mode under abnormal conditions, Transgrid controls the PSTs on AEMO's instruction.

05 / Settlements

IRSR & Settlement Changes

New IRSR Methodology — Net Trade Approach
The AEMC rule change (final determination Sep 2025, commenced Oct 2025) introduces a net trade approach to IRSR allocation across the loop.

IRSR will be allocated across three pairs of directional interconnectors: VIC-SA/SA-VIC, SA-NSW/NSW-SA, and NSW-VIC/VIC-NSW.

AEMO will allow negative IRSR to accumulate on individual legs while the loop-aggregate residue remains positive — i.e. no clamping of PEC flows when the net loop is positive. A new Loop NRM Flag (0 or 1) in market data will track this status.
Key Settlement Dates & SRA
1 October 2026 — "Loop operations start date": PEC registered as separate interconnector in AEMO dispatch systems.

1 November 2026 (backdated to 1 Oct) — "Loop settlements start date": new IRSR settlement logic active.

SRA: PEC (NSW1-SA1) will be included in the Settlement Residue Auction from launch. SRA participants will be able to hedge congestion rents on the new SA-NSW path.

Price scaling: The price-scaling algorithm will be updated to handle a transmission loop — effective August 2026.
⚠ Negative Residue Management (NRM) Change

Previously, AEMO would "clamp" interconnector flows if negative IRSR accumulated beyond a threshold. Under the new rules, AEMO will NOT clamp PEC flows when the net loop-aggregate IRSR is positive. The intent is to avoid reducing consumer benefits from the interconnector. Participants should note that negative IRSR on an individual directional leg (e.g. SA-NSW) is now permitted as long as the total loop IRSR is net positive.

06 / System Security

SAIT-RAS — What Traders Need to Know

600
MW — MAX FAST RESPONSE
SAIT-RAS can deploy up to 600 MW of proportional fast response in SA within milliseconds of a contingency detection.
📡
Monitored Inputs
Line End Open (LEO) signals on PEC and HIC corridors, synchrophasor (PMU) measurements, SA inertia level, and real-time availability of response resources.
⚡
Response Actions
Trip load, trip generation, or charge/discharge SA BESSs. Response must be predictable and fast. New SA BESS connections must demonstrate ride-through capability for loss of either interconnector.
💡 Implication for SA BESS Operators

SAIT-RAS relies on controllable SA resources. Any new connection to PEC-corridor SA infrastructure must remain connected during a non-credible loss of Heywood or PEC. If this can't be achieved, connection size or location may need to change. Early engagement with ElectraNet is essential. See the published Interface Between Battery Energy Storage Systems and SAIT RAS guidance document.
